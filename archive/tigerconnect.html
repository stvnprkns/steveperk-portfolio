TigerText (now called TigerConnect) is a HIPAA-compliant messenger app built for doctors and nurses. They had just finished up an early beta testing phase and needed a complete redesign for their mobile app (I became the entire design department.)

HIPAA can be a challenge, especially for a medical messaging tool that works both for internal communications (Doctors ‚≠§ Nurses) as well as external (Doctors ‚≠§ Patients). The biggest challenge wasn't just regulatory. It was also a behavioral issue, Medical professionals didn't want an app that felt like a consumer tool (WhatsApp, FB Messenger, or SMS). This forced me to think about what a 'professional messaging app' should look and feel like.

Also, I had to account for some unique situations:

üí¨ Killing Pagers

Every doctor I spoke with said that pagers are a pain-in-the-butt legacy device and they need to go away. Our mission was to make beepers obsolete in hospitals, which at the time, 98%¬†of hospitals still used pagers.

My focus was on building a tool around the most basic behaviors of sending a message. It needed to be simple and fast since E.R. doctors don't have a lot of time to dilly on their phones. Design-wise, I used Apple and Android design guidelines. I created a view for internal messages and a separate view for external ones with prompts to make sure they were sending the right information to the right person.

I also designed an entire admin platform for managing messages, storing documents, editing permissions...and a lot more.

üêÖ Where is it now?
‚Äç
After TigerText launched, the company onboarded over a hundred hospitals and continued to grow incredibly fast. The success of the launch helped the company raise $103M in funding and grow from 25 people to over 150 employees.

TigerText has since changed its name to TigerConnect. There are now over 6,000 medical organizations on the app and more than 10 million messages are sent daily.

Also today, just 5 years after the launch, 20% of hospitals use some kind of mobile technology to replace the dreaded pager.

The Work

Research Checkups

I knew that understanding our users was key to creating a better healthcare messaging app. So, despite being a bit tight on cash, I'd grab a bunch of $10 Starbucks Gift Cards on my lunch break and head to the nearby hospitals. There, between their smoke breaks, I'd chat up doctors and nurses, ask them to test our app, and get their thoughts. This hands-on approach helped us fine-tune the app to fit the unique needs of medical professionals. The feedback we gathered in those candid conversations played a big part in making TigerText a hit.

I talked to so many nurses and doctors that year, and let me tell you, they're incredible. Hard-working, empathetic, and genuinely wonderful people. Total heroes in my book.

The App

The app was built mobile-first, with an emphasis on tablet usage. The designs included the ability for doctors to select between multiple hospitals, create and send group messages, as well as integrating secure 3rd party applications to be able to send notifications about patients and procedures.

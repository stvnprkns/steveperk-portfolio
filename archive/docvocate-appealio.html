üîÆ Overview

In the complex world of healthcare, where lives and money are on the line, insurance appeals play a crucial role. They're like behind-the-scenes negotiations between healthcare providers and insurance companies to figure out how much the provider will charge, what the insurance company will pay, and ultimately, the financial outcome for the provider.

Our agency was chosen to create the first version of Appealio's app, which uses artificial intelligence to help hospitals and medical offices automate insurance appeals more efficiently. It's a game-changer for the industry.

ü©∫ The Challenges
‚Äç
The biggest design challenge was a regulatory one: HIPAA. Our team needed to ensure that patient data never gets exposed, while still allowing solution necessary information to be accessed and shared intuitively with the hospital staff and insurance companies.

Every appeal requires the medical provider to write a letter explaining the procedure, doctor and patient information, the success of the procedure, and why the medical provider feels they need a certain percentage of insurance coverage, among other things.
‚Äç
To process appeals, healthcare providers not only need to store patient information but since many appeals go through several review cycles, they also need the ability to go back and see previous notes and updates they‚Äôve made.

üìú The Mission

Our goal was to make the appeal process faster, and smarter using AI.

Currently, hospitals organize appeals exclusively by the due date and work on the ones with the closest date. Appealio digitized this process and created a workflow that organizes appeals not only by the due date, but the ease of filing, and value (since high-cost appeals are way more important to the hospital). They then move these appeals to be submitted, re-appealed, and finally, closed.

The biggest time-suck for hospital admins was hand-typing appeal letters. We used A.I. to create customized appeal letters that automatically import patient and procedure information and can be completely customized. Appealio stores version controls of past appeals, patient documents like X-rays, and doctor notes.

Sitemap

Our client came to us with an idea. We had to figure out everything else. To help get us all aligned on how their product would work and what it would contain, we created a sitemap.

The sitemap lays out everything the application could contain and everything it could help users do‚Äîonboarding, viewing appeals, creating a new appeal, managing everything through a dashboard, etc.

Guerilla Research

In the agency world research is often a luxury. But we're not your average design team. When faced with the challenge of creating Appealio, we took matters into our own hands.

We pounded the pavement around our cities, visiting medical offices and hospitals unannounced. We wanted to do more than just collect data; we wanted to hear directly from the professionals dealing with insurance appeals.

Our candid conversations provided a wealth of insights. We learned about the daily struggles, triumphs, and desires of those handling insurance appeals. These unfiltered exchanges provided direction for our path forward, ensuring Appealio would truly meet the needs of its users.

Appeals Dashboard

The process most medical providers use to sort insurance claims is to literally stack them up in order by the due date.

I had my team drive around their town interviewing medical providers, like eye doctors and dentists, to figure out what would be better details to sort these documents by. Then we fed those insights into the A.I. to create a smart document sort and search.

Filing an Appeal

The process for filing an appeal is inputting patient information, denial information (the reason the original submission was denied), a letter about the procedure, and why it should be accepted, uploading patient documents (CT scans, notes, etc.) and lastly reviewing before submission.

Appeal Letter

Every appeal requires a letter written about the patient and procedure. This is very time-consuming, so using the A.I. our development team built, we created an automated letter-writing tool that uses templates (written by the doctor) and patient data to write appeal letters.

The two biggest tasks that were necessary for the A.I. to work were:

‚Üí Develop Templates & Classification
Templates are maps of content for documents‚Äîlike a tax form. These help the A.I. identify key areas or common titles in the document to extract and verify the information. We created multiple data extraction methods because not all forms have the same layout or information (think invoices).

After a template is created, the auditor will select a grouping of templates and create what EY calls 'Classifications.' This allows the auditor to upload folders of related documents to be read and processed together.

‚Üí Improve Document Flow Management
Every document goes through a linear process (Upload ‚Üí A.I. Extraction ‚Üí Manual Validation ‚Üí Export ‚Üí Archive). Our team's goal was to design this part of the tool to be as simple as using a C.R.M. (Client Relationship Manager). Since accountants were skeptical of the accuracy of the A.I., our team created a confidence score with an easy one-click action check for the auditor.

With a singular platform, the A.I. is able to learn document scenarios across multiple departments. It also matches employees to specific document types, making it easier for auditors to connect to subject matter experts within their own organization, whenever they need guidance.

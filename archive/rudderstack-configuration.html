Idea in Brief

üîê Overview
‚Äç
In the world of Customer Data Platforms (CDPs), the foundation of everything is the ingestion of data from sources (such as websites, apps, etc.) where data is being created and sending that data to a destination where the data can be used (such as a warehouse for storage, an analytics tool, or a platform like Facebook Ads).

To send data from a source to a destination, customers must set up all the necessary configurations including how, what, when, and where the data will be sent. While this may seem simple on the surface, it is not so straightforward in execution. There are numerous independent factors to consider. For example, almost every destination requires around 40 configuration settings, many of which have downstream impacts. RudderStack currently has over 200+ destinations that need to be accounted for.

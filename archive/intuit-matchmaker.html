Quickbooks helps small business owners and entrepreneurs manage expenses and their bookkeeping. The MatchMaker project was an experiment to connect these customers with real CPAs to help them with their books, and more importantly, their taxes.

ü™ê Discovery
‚Äç
We started our project discovery with these facts in mind: 1-in-5 small businesses fail within the first year, and that number moves to 1-in-2 in 5 years.

We wanted to see if we could find a reason behind that number and a way to save hundreds of thousands of businesses every year.


üî¨ Research

3-days-a-week, I pounded the pavement of every business community in Dallas (Deep Ellum, Bishop Arts, Greenville Ave., Downtown Plano, etc.). I went door to door asking if I could talk with the owner or general manager of every business. Most owners were incredibly open to talk.

I spent on average 15 minutes talking with each owner ¬†trying to get their answers to these ¬†4 questions:
‚Äç
‚Üí What was their biggest struggle in starting and running their business?

‚Üí If the business was to ever fail, what reason would it be for?

‚Üí How important was their CPA and/or bookkeeper to their success?

‚Üí And what kept them up at night?

‚Äç
üé¢ The Process

We found that statistically if a business had both a CPA and bookkeeper they were 3x more likely to make it past the 5-year mark than a business that didn't.

The hypothesis we set out to prove was that if we could connect a business owner to qualified CPAs through Quickbooks they'd be more likely to save money on taxes, comply with tax laws, be more efficient with their money, and continue operating 5 years from now.

‚Äç
üõ† What we built

We created multiple experiments with versions in Quickbooks, TurboTax, and one separate from Intuit altogether. Our goal was to find an effective way to match businesses to CPAs. We built a common back-end tool that consisted of:

- A matching tool
- Real-time messaging
- Payment processing
- And connected to our tax platforms.

The Work

Research

Throughout this project, I interviewed over 100 small business owners, entrepreneurs, makers, and chefs. As a result, I learned that a majority of businesses did have CPAs and bookkeepers, many they knew through family or friends.

There were a few though that didn't have a CPA or a bookkeeper‚Äîand after talking with them, it became apparent that they didn't understand the value of paying a professional. They felt they were doing it just fine themselves. These people became our beta group.

Online Matchmaking

One of the key insights I gathered from interviews was there was no such thing as a one-size-fits-all CPA. Our users wanted specialists who understood their industry as they did.¬†
‚Äç
To make these users feel comfortable, we started the CPA selection process with a series of filters. After the user imputed what was important to them, we presented¬† them with a list of CPAs the application had "hand-selected."

Starting the Engagement

Once a customer was connected with an accountant they went through the 'Consultation' tool. Since this was a lean experiment, we looked for the fastest, highest-value way to implement this feature. We chose to integrate it as a widget in Quickbooks since most small business owners already relied on the program to do their bookkeeping.

Design, while in many cases seems to be this elusive, subjective experience. There are so many instances where something just 'feels right.' But as design leaders, we really want to find ways to measure the effectiveness of design through quantitative or qualitative means. When it comes to features we can always look at scoring user journeys, the number of conversions, or the output of something, but when it comes to design as a whole within the product how do we know if it's really getting better or we're just doing a lot of work that 'feels good?'

NPS is a very executive number. There have been countless articles about the faults with NPS, yet we still continue to do it and taut the number going up. NPS has some overlap with design and is really intended to get a broad strokes measure of how the company as a whole is improving. But design is only a segment of this and there is not often a direct measure for design. So we wanted to find something to really focus exclusively on the product experience.

The design process

At RudderStack, our engineering team is based in India, posing a unique challenge due to the 12.5-hour time difference. To address this, we've developed a hybrid approach that combines the best of waterfall and agile methodologies.

Our design process follows a broad structure: Planning, Kickoff, Research and Design, Engineering, and QA. But here's the exciting part: within each phase, we sprinkle in agile sprints to execute our grand plan, adding spontaneity to keep things exciting!

During the Research phase, the design team comes together to plan and conduct interviews and data analysis to understand our customers' needs, behaviors, and quirks, enabling us to tackle the problem head-on.

Next, in the concepting phase, UX Writing and Information Architecture collaborate to craft a compelling narrative and organize information intuitively.

Finally, our talented UI designer brings the vision to life using our design library, Anchor, for finishing touches.

But the secret sauce is collaboration. While we have our own areas of expertise, we constantly bounce ideas off each other and work as a tight-knit team. Our IA excels in both wires and writing, our UI designer can handle IA work, and our writer spots UI inconsistencies, enhancing our skills across disciplines.

Uncovering the opportunity

While the CDP industry traditionally lacked enthusiasm for design, we saw untapped potential. This presented a golden opportunity to make design a cornerstone of RudderStack’s value proposition, awakening the industry to the benefits of integrating design more deeply into business strategy and product development.

Navigating the challenge

The industry’s entrenched indifference towards design posed a formidable barrier, mirrored within RudderStack by a culture yet to fully embrace design’s strategic importance. Bridging this gap demanded more than just skill—it required a paradigm shift in perception, fostering an environment where design was not just seen as a production role. But as a partner in product development and business strategy.

This required building trust, and a real-partnership with cross-functional teams.

The Vision

RudderStack’s ambition was to stand-out in the competitive CDP industry using the power of design, setting a new standard for what was possible. This vision was expansive, seeing design not just as a tool for beautification but as a strategic asset capable of unlocking innovation, enhancing user satisfaction, and carving out a competitive edge.

Our goal was to weave design into the very fabric of RudderStack's culture and operations, approaching every challenge through the lens of, customer-first, design thinking.

The transformation of RudderStack into a design-centric organization was a meticulously crafted journey, underpinned by strategic decisions and collaborative efforts aimed at weaving design into the fabric of our culture and operations.

Assembling a Visionary Team

‍Our first step was akin to gathering a crew for a voyage into uncharted waters. We sought individuals not just for their skills, but for their ability to work independently and push boundaries. This diverse group of 'T-shaped' talents brought together UI Systems Designers, Information Architects, UX Writers, Branding Designers, and Web Designers—each an expert in their domain yet versatile enough to contribute across the spectrum.

Cultivating a Design-First Culture

‍Changing the organizational DNA was paramount. Through a series of interdisciplinary workshops, we dismantled silos and fostered a shared understanding of design’s pivotal role in driving innovation. These sessions, illuminated by the sparks of creativity and collaboration, laid the groundwork for a culture where design is revered as a critical business function.

Refining Our Design Processes

‍With our team and culture in place, attention turned to the mechanics of design within RudderStack. We introduced Design QA and Research phases at the inception of every project, ensuring decisions were data-driven and user-centric. Regular Design Critiques and Shares became our rhythm, allowing for continuous feedback and iteration. The creation of a seamless workflow bridged the gap between Product and Marketing, ensuring a cohesive brand experience.

The strategic elevation of design within RudderStack led to significant business outcomes, painting a vivid picture of success through improved efficiency, quality, and user satisfaction.

Elevating Product Quality

Our rigorous Design QA and collaborative processes led to a remarkable reduction in design-related bugs. This commitment to quality not only polished our products but also reinforced our brand's reputation for reliability and excellence in the market.

Enhancing User Satisfaction

‍Perhaps most telling was the significant leap in our System Usability Score (SUS), an industry standard tool for assessing a product's usability, offering a series of ten questions that measure a user's perception of ease of use and satisfaction. It provides a quick, comprehensive snapshot of a product’s usability from the user's standpoint.

Over the course of 3 quarters we improved our SUS score from 62 → 68.

The quick rise in RudderStack's SUS score was a clear indicator of the success of our design-first strategy. This metric captured the direct impact of our design approach on user experience, demonstrating how thoughtful design can lead to a more intuitive and satisfying product, and therefore more users paying for the product and a higher customer retention rate. The improvement in our SUS score not only highlighted the enhanced quality of our design work but also aligned with our strategic goal of setting new benchmarks for usability and user satisfaction in the CDP industry.

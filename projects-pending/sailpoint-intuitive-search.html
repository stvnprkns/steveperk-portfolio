Before jumping in I encouraged my team to start with discovery research (who's using search, what are they searching for, how are they searching, and are they getting the expected results?)

The team gathered data from dozens of research sessions and usage data.
We learned that very few customers knew how to write search queries in syntax. More surprisingly 80% of searches were saved searches that the customer uses daily. And in most cases the customer had a developer write the query and they just saved it.

"Search is really, really tough to work with. For something as critical as a search query to certify access, it is scary how easy it is for a bad search that could lead to an invalid campaign."

- SailPoint Customer

‚Äçüïπ Simplifying Search
‚Äç
Originally when we started the process there was talk of expanding search to encompass and/or commands and attribute grouping. But after diving into discovery we determined that we actually needed to go more simple with search. So we started with the most basic search requests (what made up the majority of search entries) and built up the complexity.

We found that the ideal solution for all customers was to create a filter builder tool with search to essentially narrow the results by removing obvious chunks of unneeded results.

After we launched the redesigned Search experience, usage of Search increased by 220%. But the end result wasn't to get more customers to search, but to get to the information needed faster.

Time of search-to-result decreased by more than 70% and satisfaction scores and reviews increased dramatically.
